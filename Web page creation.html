Tajmahal

The Taj Mahal is located on the right bank of the Yamuna River in a vast Mughal garden that encompasses nearly 17 hectares,in the Agra District in Uttar Pradesh . It was built by Mughal Emperor Shah Jahan in memory of his Wife Mumtaz Mahal with constraction starting in 1632 AD and completed in 1648 AD, with the mosque,the guest house and the main gateway on the south,the outer courtyard and its cloisters were added subsequently and completed in 1653 AD.The existence of several historical and Quaranic inscriptions in Arabic script have facilitated setting the chronology of Taj Mahal.


constraction

The Taj Mahal is a perfect symmetrical planned building ,with an emphasis of bilateral symmetry along a central axis on which the main features are placed. The building materail used is brick-in-lime mortat veneered with red sandstone and marble and inlay work of precious/semiprecious stones.The mosque and the guest house in the Taj Mahal complex are built of red sandstone in constract to the marble tomb in the centre .Both the building have a large platform over the terrace at their front.
